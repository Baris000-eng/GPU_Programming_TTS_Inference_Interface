Ducks are incredibly adaptable, outgoing, and social aquatic birds recognized for their webbed feet and waterproof feathers. These unique features allow them to navigate their habitats easily, whether they are foraging for seeds and insects in shallow wetlands or diving deep underwater for food. Beyond their swimming abilities, ducks are socially complex animals known to mate for a breeding season and live in flocks. Many species are highly adapted to their surroundings, with anatomical features like specialized beaks, known as bills, that help them scoop up food or filter it directly from the mud.
